#+title: cools-acute-2008

Cools et al. showed in a [[id:a13dcc9a-91d5-46a1-aa2b-4c960626f1f7][reversal learning]] task that humans tend to underestimate the probability of failure compared to success.
If their diets are modified in order to deplete their serotonin levels their estimation of the odds of failure becomes more accurate - they become more pessimistic.

https://www.nature.com/articles/1301598.pdf
